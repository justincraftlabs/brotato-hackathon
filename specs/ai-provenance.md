# AI Provenance

> For the 2026 AI Hackathon Criterion 2 ("AI-Native Development Lifecycle").
> Documents which artefacts were AI-drafted vs. human-authored vs. AI-draft +
> human-edited, so judges can verify developer control at every stage.
>
> **Guiding principle (OSD deck, slide 18):** AI drafts, humans govern
> normative artefacts. Every ⚙️-marked artefact below was reviewed,
> accepted or corrected, and owned by a human.

## Legend

- 🤖 **AI-drafted, human-accepted as-is** — Claude generated it, human read it line-by-line, accepted without edits
- ⚙️ **AI-draft + human-edited** — Claude produced a first pass; human rewrote or restructured it before accepting
- 👤 **Human-authored** — written by a person; AI may have been consulted but did not write the text
- 🔁 **Iterated** — multiple AI passes with prompt refinement between them

## Rejected AI suggestions (honest log)

Slide 8 of the OSD deck: "Agents draft, humans govern." Below are suggestions Claude proposed that were rejected, with the reasoning:

1. **Rate limiting on `/api/ai/*`** — Claude recommended `express-rate-limit` in the initial security review. Deferred to post-MVP: no auth means no way to fairly key per-user, and hackathon demo doesn't need it. Captured in `specs/security-review.md` "Follow-ups before a real deploy".
2. **`helmet` middleware by default** — Same reasoning. Noted in security review as a pre-deploy item.
3. **Zod `.strict()` everywhere** — Rejected because our envelope pattern relies on `.optional().default(...)` for a few appliance fields; stricter parsing would have broken the setup wizard for legacy rooms.
4. **Switching to Prisma** — Claude proposed mid-project. Rejected: Mongoose works and the team knows it; switching ORMs mid-hackathon is pure risk with no demo-value payoff.
5. **Pre-rendering `/tips`** — Claude suggested `export const dynamic = "force-dynamic"` for the `useSearchParams` prerender issue. It doesn't actually fix the problem (the suspense boundary is required). Ticket opened as a follow-up.

## How a reviewer can verify

1. Every row marked ⚙️ or 🔁 has at least one human in the commit author or co-author. Check `git log --follow <path>`.
2. The "Rejected AI suggestions" are actual decisions made during this hackathon — not fabricated retrospectively. Cross-reference with `RETROSPECTIVE.md`.
3. For ✅/❌ rows in `specs/verification-matrix.md`, every Evidence Path points at a test that actually exists and runs in CI.
